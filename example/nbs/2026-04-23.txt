OPEN-CONVERSATION PROMPTS (one per objective, to sharpen with your team)
========================================================================

--- Objective 1: Complete the AWS Migration ---

I've been framing this one as "Complete the AWS Migration" -- wrapping source-data
migration, team-owned process migration, and Partner-agnostic alternative views
into a single year-end outcome. But that's a utilitarian title. Would you phrase
it as something broader and more aspirational -- "Modernize Our Data
Infrastructure," "Become a Cloud-Native Data Team," "Retire Legacy Data
Platforms" -- or does the plain migration framing fit better because it's
concrete and measurable? Curious how you'd say it in your own words.

On the benefits, my draft has: retiring legacy Hadoop/SAS compute costs,
unblocking analytical users locked out of direct Partner data, and giving
downstream AI a single platform to target. Which of those would actually make
your leadership lean in, and what am I missing -- cost savings with a dollar
figure, a deprecation deadline, a specific use case that's been stuck for
quarters? If you imagine a three-minute year-end readout where you get to claim
"we delivered on AWS migration," what's the one headline number or story you'd
most want to be able to say?


--- Objective 2: Establish Single Source of Truth with Clear Ownership and Full Compliance ---

I've bundled three legacy objectives together here -- SSOT for critical
datasets, named ownership across the lifecycle, and full GDMS/CDE compliance --
because to me they all roll up to "data you can trust, with someone accountable
for it." Does that bundling feel right to you, or are these really three
different conversations with three different sponsors that I'm artificially
merging? If you'd rather keep them separate or reshuffle, which splits make
sense?

Benefits I drafted: ending "whose number is right?" debates, issue routing in
hours instead of weeks, and passing enterprise/regulator audits on first look.
Which of those lands hardest for your audience -- the business-user pain, the
internal efficiency win, or the regulatory story? And if you had to pick one
dataset that would make this objective feel real in a readout ("this is our
SSOT, here's who owns it, here's the GDMS evidence"), what would it be?


--- Objective 3: Deliver Self-Service, High-Quality Data for Analysts ---

I've been framing this as "analysts can find, understand, and trust data
without asking us" -- pulling together Marketplace adoption, consistent
definitions/shapes, and the move from detective to preventive DQ. Does that
match how you think about the analyst pain point today, or would you lead with
a sharper problem -- "analysts spend 80% of their time hunting data,"
"downstream reports break twice a quarter because of upstream DQ," something
like that?

The benefits I drafted lean on improvements: time-to-data from days to minutes,
defects blocked upstream, fewer re-pulls per ask. But those are only as
compelling as the baseline. Do you already track any of these (Marketplace
adoption, DQ ticket volume, analyst NPS/survey, re-pull rate) that we could
anchor the objective to? And who is "the analyst" we're serving -- CFDM,
business-unit analysts, risk teams -- because the benefit story changes a lot
by persona. Whose experience would you most want to point to?


--- Objective 4: Unify Analytics and Validation Inside Databricks ---

My current framing is narrow on purpose: "run end-to-end validation inside
Databricks, with a feedback loop to the platform team." The pain I'm capturing
is that one validation cycle today requires SAS server + AWS CLI + local
scripts stitched together. But I'm not sure I've aimed high enough -- do you
want this scoped to validation only, or should it be broader ("Databricks as
the single workbench for the team") and cover all analytics, not just
validation? The wider scope is a bigger story but a longer commitment.

Benefits I had: no more tool-hopping per cycle, platform evolves around actual
USCB use cases, single pane of glass for results. Different audiences care
about different pieces -- analysts want the tool-hopping to stop, leadership
wants efficiency numbers, the Databricks platform team wants the feedback
loop. If we only get to optimize this objective's benefit story for one
audience, who is it, and what would they most want to hear?


--- Objective 5: Embed AI Across Data Operations and Team Workflows ---

This one mixes two distinct AI bets: AI inside the platform (self-healing
pipelines, AI monitoring for completeness, auto-documented processes) and AI
in team workflows (Copilot prompts for onboarding, metrics tracking, decks,
etc.). I've been tying them together as "AI making the team more leveraged,"
but they have different owners, different timelines, and different risk
profiles. Do you see them as one story or two -- and if one, what's the thread
that actually connects them for your audience?

For benefits I have: less data-ops toil, hours reclaimed on recurring
deliverables, and a strong "security guardrails" story. "AI embedded
everywhere" is easy to say and hard to prove, so I'd rather anchor this to one
concrete deliverable you'd be proud to show at year-end. What would that be --
a self-healing pipeline running on a low-stakes dataset, three Copilot prompts
in daily team use, a specific monitoring task fully automated, something else?
If we pick one flagship, what is it?
